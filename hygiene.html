Pet Hygiene & Care

Keep your pet clean, healthy, and happy with our recommended hygiene practices

Bath Time Basics

How to keep your pet clean without stressing them out

How Often?

Most dogs should be bathed every 1-3 months, depending on their coat type and activity level. Overbathing can strip natural oils from their skin.

Water Temperature

Use lukewarm water that's comfortable to the touch. Test it on your wrist first, just as you would for a baby.

Pet-Safe Products

Only use shampoos specifically formulated for dogs. Human products can disrupt their skin pH balance and cause irritation.

Bathing Frequency by Breed

Coat Type Recommended Frequency Special Considerations
Short coat (Beagle, Labrador) Every 2-3 months Brush weekly to remove loose fur
Double coat (Husky, German Shepherd) Every 3-4 months Regular brushing, avoid over-bathing
Long coat (Shih Tzu, Yorkshire Terrier) Every 4-6 weeks Regular grooming to prevent matting
Curly coat (Poodle, Bichon Frise) Every 3-4 weeks Professional grooming recommended
Wrinkly skin (Bulldog, Shar Pei) Every 2-4 weeks Clean between folds regularly

Dental Hygiene

Maintaining your pet's oral health prevents disease and bad breath

Brushing

Aim to brush your pet's teeth 2-3 times per week with pet-specific toothpaste. Never use human toothpaste, which contains harmful ingredients.

Dental Chews

Provide dental chews or toys to help reduce plaque buildup. Look for products with the Veterinary Oral Health Council (VOHC) seal.

Professional Cleaning

Schedule professional dental cleanings with your vet once a year to prevent periodontal disease.

Signs of Dental Problems

  • Bad breath
  • Yellow or brown buildup on teeth
  • Bleeding or swollen gums
  • Difficulty eating or decreased appetite
  • Excessive drooling
  • Pawing at the mouth

Eye & Ear Care

Regular maintenance to prevent infections

Eye Cleaning

Wipe away discharge with a damp cotton ball, working from the corner of the eye outward. Use separate cotton balls for each eye.

Ear Cleaning

Clean the outer ear canal weekly with a veterinary-approved ear cleaner. Never insert anything into the ear canal.

Warning Signs

Watch for redness, swelling, excessive discharge, or pawing at eyes/ears. These require veterinary attention.

Nail Care

Proper nail maintenance for your pet's comfort

Trimming Schedule

Most pets need nail trims every 3-4 weeks. If you can hear nails clicking on the floor, they're too long.

Trimming Technique

Cut just the tip of the nail, avoiding the quick (pink area with blood vessels). Use proper pet nail clippers.

Emergency Care

If you cut the quick, apply styptic powder immediately to stop bleeding. If bleeding continues for more than 5 minutes, contact your vet.
