Pet Emergency Care

Be prepared for pet emergencies with our quick reference guide

IMPORTANT NOTICE

This guide provides basic information for common pet emergencies. Always consult with a veterinarian as soon as possible for proper medical care.

Call your emergency vet clinic or pet poison hotline immediately if your pet is in distress.

Recognize Emergency Signs

These symptoms require immediate veterinary attention:

Cardiac & Respiratory

  • Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
  • Blue or pale gums
  • Collapse or fainting
  • Severe coughing or choking
  • Weak pulse or irregular heartbeat

Body Temperature

  • Extremely high temperature (over 103째F/39.4째C)
  • Low temperature (below 99째F/37.2째C)
  • Heat stroke symptoms (excessive panting, bright red gums)
  • Hypothermia (shivering, lethargy)

Bleeding & Injury

  • Severe bleeding that doesn't stop within 5 minutes
  • Deep wounds or punctures
  • Broken bones or limping
  • Trauma from falls or being hit by a car
  • Eye injuries

Poisoning

  • Ingestion of toxic substances
  • Vomiting or diarrhea with blood
  • Seizures or tremors
  • Excessive drooling
  • Sudden weakness or collapse

Digestive Issues

  • Bloated, hard abdomen
  • Unsuccessful attempts to vomit
  • Severe or persistent vomiting
  • Not eating or drinking for 24+ hours
  • Inability to urinate

Neurological

  • Seizures or convulsions
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Severe disorientation
  • Head tilt or loss of balance
  • Paralysis or inability to stand

First Aid for Common Emergencies

Bleeding

  1. Apply direct pressure to the wound using a clean cloth or gauze.
  2. If blood soaks through, add another layer without removing the first.
  3. Elevate the wound if possible.
  4. Apply a pressure bandage if bleeding continues.
  5. Seek immediate veterinary care.

Poisoning

  1. Identify the poison if possible and keep packaging.
  2. Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a vet or poison control.
  3. Contact pet poison hotline: (888) 426-4435 or your emergency vet.
  4. Follow their instructions precisely.
  5. Take your pet to the vet immediately with the substance packaging.

Seizures

  1. Keep your pet away from furniture or stairs where they could be injured.
  2. Do not put anything in your pet's mouth.
  3. Time the seizure - note start and end time.
  4. Keep the room quiet and dark if possible.
  5. Contact your vet once the seizure has ended.

Heatstroke

  1. Move your pet to a cool area immediately.
  2. Apply cool (not cold) water to the body, especially neck, armpits, and groin.
  3. Place wet towels on your pet and direct a fan toward them.
  4. Offer small amounts of cool water to drink.
  5. Transport to the vet even if symptoms improve.

Emergency Transport

If you need to transport your pet during an emergency:

  • Call the vet ahead so they can prepare for your arrival
  • For small pets, use a carrier lined with a towel
  • For larger dogs, use a rigid surface like a board as a stretcher
  • Muzzle your pet if necessary for safety (do not muzzle if vomiting or having breathing difficulties)
  • Minimize movement if you suspect broken bones
  • Keep the pet warm with blankets
  • Drive carefully - having someone else drive while you attend to the pet is ideal
